This study proposed a response-supervised, geometry-constrained graph sequence
framework for spatially explicit representation of rock--TBM interaction.  The
method formalises TSP voxels and TBM surface components as heterogeneous spatial
entities, screens candidate relations by geometric compatibility, and projects
learned relevance back to TBM surface and chainage views.

The mileage-ordered evaluation provides a direct test on a later continuous
tunnel interval.  The proposed framework obtains MAE 0.435, RMSE 0.545, and
$R^2=-0.463$.  Its prediction performance is comparable to the sequence
baselines, but no statistically significant advantage is established.  The
random-edge ablation performs better on the small future test set, so the
current results also do not demonstrate a predictive benefit from the
geometry-constrained topology.

The saved checkpoint nevertheless produces positively clustered TBM-surface
relevance (mean Moran's $I=0.529$) and a strong inverse chainage-local
association with TSP velocity (Pearson $r=-0.988$).  Component differentiation
is weak (mean CV\,$=0.018$), and the geology correlation may be confounded by
spatial trend.  These results support the availability of spatial diagnostics,
not the stronger claims of physical interaction recovery, geological causality,
or operational risk estimation.

The main contribution is therefore a spatial entity-relation representation
and an auditable route from response supervision to surface-based diagnostics.
Future work should establish multi-seed stability, spatially detrended geology
validation, topology and label permutation controls, threshold sensitivity,
and cross-project forward generalisation before stronger accuracy or physical
interpretability claims are made.
