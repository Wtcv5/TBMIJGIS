This study proposed a response-supervised, geometry-constrained graph sequence
framework for spatially explicit rock--TBM interaction representation. The
method formalises TSP-derived rock voxels and TBM surface components as
heterogeneous spatial entities, screens candidate rock--machine relations by
geometric compatibility, and aggregates learned relevance into component- and
chainage-resolved diagnostic views.

The revised experiments clarify the strength and boundary of the contribution.
Across BSLL DyK1017+205 and SJLS Dyk1252+411, the framework remains competitive
with temporal baselines but does not establish a broad, statistically
detectable accuracy improvement. BSLL mainly supports the multi-step and
diagnostic-interpretation use case, while SJLS provides the clearest small
case-specific geometry-related prediction difference. The consistent evidence is interpretive:
all three saved checkpoints produce spatially organised diagnostic relevance, and
the degree-control checks indicate that relevance is not simply a proxy for
candidate-edge count.

The main contribution is therefore not a claim of broad forecasting improvement
but a spatial entity-relation representation and an auditable route from
response supervision to component- and chainage-resolved diagnostic relevance.
Future work should test
multi-seed stability, label-shuffle controls, threshold sensitivity, spatially
detrended geology association, and additional tunnel cases before broader
claims are made about contact-condition recovery or operational risk
estimation.
