## Baseline Argument Extracted From Current Draft

- Problem the paper solves: monitoring-only TBM sequence representations do not
  explicitly organize rock location, machine-component geometry, and local
  interaction evolution.
- Main gap: lack of a spatially explicit and geometrically plausible interaction
  representation that can still be learned from practical continuous monitoring
  response -- a geographic-information representation problem.
- Design response: a geometry-constrained heterogeneous graph sequence supervised
  by TBM response.
- Intended evidence path: baseline comparison, structural ablation, and
  projection-based interpretation outputs.
- Core boundary: learned edge relevance is response-consistent, not a physically
  validated contact-force field.
- GIScience contribution: advances spatial entity modeling, relation plausibility
  under incomplete observability, and interpretable geospatial learning.
