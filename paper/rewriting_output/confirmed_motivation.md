| Confirmed motivation statement | Existing TBM monitoring sequence models can predict or correlate excavation responses, but they do not spatially resolve where rock--TBM interactions occur, which TBM components are implicated, or how these interactions evolve along chainage. This is fundamentally a geographic-information representation problem: heterogeneous spatial entities (rock voxels, machine surface components) with different spatial support, physical meaning, and observation granularity must be organized into a representation that preserves local spatial structure, relation plausibility, and excavation evolution. The paper therefore aims to build a spatially explicit, geometrically plausible, response-supervised graph sequence representation that formalizes spatial entities, constrains candidate relations by geometric compatibility, and projects learned relevance back to interpretable spatial views -- contributing a spatial representation and interpretable geospatial learning framework, not merely a prediction tool. |
