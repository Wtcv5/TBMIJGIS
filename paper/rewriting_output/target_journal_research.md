## Fit assessment for the current paper concept

### Why the topic fits

- The abstract is method-centered rather than project-report-centered.
- The contribution is framed around spatial representation, graph construction, interaction learning, and interpretable geospatial outputs.
- The framework combines geological sensing, machine geometry, graph learning, and spatial interpretation, which matches IJGIS interest in computational and representational advances for geographic information.

### Main fit risks

- If the paper reads primarily as a tunnel-engineering application paper with limited GIScience contribution, IJGIS fit weakens.
- If TSP, shield geometry, and monitoring streams are presented only as engineering variables without a strong geographic-information representation argument, reviewers may see the work as domain-specific ML rather than GIScience.
- The paper should make clear why the voxelized geology, chainage-referenced interaction graph, and spatial projection outputs constitute a geographic information representation problem.

## Writing implications for this paper

- The Introduction should foreground the geographic-information and spatial-representation gap, not only TBM operational risk.
- Methods should formalize the spatial entities, relations, constraints, and projection space clearly.
- Results should include both model-comparison evidence and spatial interpretability evidence.
- Discussion should connect the method to broader GIScience ideas: spatial entity modeling, relation plausibility, representation under incomplete observability, and interpretable geospatial learning.
