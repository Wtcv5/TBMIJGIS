1. Introduction
Tunnels, as an essential component of transportation infrastructure, demand high standards in
construction quality and safety management (Cui, Broere, and Lin 2021; Zhu et al. 2024). The con­
cept of digital twins aims to map the real world physical entities to the virtual models, effectively
enabling the monitoring, simulation, prediction, diagnosis, and decision-making. Applying digital
twin technology to tunnel excavation enables intelligent construction, playing a critical role in

ensuring construction safety and advancing intelligent building techniques (Guo et al. 2025; Tao
et al. 2018; Wu et al. 2023; Zhu et al. 2024). In practice, complex geological conditions often
lead to significant geometric deformation of the tunnel profile, affecting its stability (Cai, Hu,
and Ma 2021; Zhang et al. 2024). As a result, tunnel profile deformation prediction becomes a cru­
cial aspect of tunnel engineering. Monitoring and accurately predicting these deformations can pro­
vide a basis and guidance for tunnel construction risk management, construction plan adjustments,
and decision-making. These measures are essential for ensuring the quality of tunnel engineering.
   Currently, common methods for tunnel construction deformation predicting mainly include the
gray model method, finite element analysis and deep learning. Gray model method is a mathematical
method for predicting and analysing uncertain events. It allows for modelling with limited data and
offers low computational costs (Chen and Wang 2020; Tien 2011; Xie 2022). However, the defor­
mation data of tunnel construction often exhibits significant spatiotemporal variability. Gray model
method relies on limited historical data for analysis, and the prediction accuracy is limited. Moreover,
the gray model method cannot effectively capture the complex relationship within extensive exca­
vation data (Fei et al. 2020; He, Armaghani, and Lai 2023; Huang, Ninic, and Zhang 2021; Mahmood­
zadeh, Nejati, and Mohammadi 2022). Finite element analysis can decompose the complex tunnel
continuum into simpler finite elements. By constructing equations for these elements, the tunnel
profile deformation analysis and prediction results can be obtained. It is capable to handle complex
geometries, boundary conditions, and material properties, making it suitable for analysing composite
materials and structures during tunnel construction (Cuomo et al. 2022; La Russa et al. 2022; Liu et al.
2023). However, in tunnel engineering, the geological conditions are highly variable. It takes a lot of
time to establish a finite element model of tunnel excavation process that integrates elements such as
tunnel stratigraphy, groundwater levels, and geological structures (Li et al. 2024a; Sun et al. 2022; Ye,
Zhao, and Wang 2021). Moreover, the amount of calculation required for finite element analysis is
very large, especially when the model involves a substantial number of nodes and elements, which
requires high-performance computing resources (Fukuda et al. 2021; Zhang et al. 2022).
   In recent years, the maturity of deep learning theory and its applications have provided new
opportunities for solving tunnel-related issues. Deep learning is based on a large amount of data
for calculation. It not only relies on data for training and optimization, but also can automatically
learn and extract useful information from data (Ganaie et al. 2021; Reichstein et al. 2019). It is a
typical data-driven method, which is particularly good at identifying and learning complex patterns
and relationships within data. Meanwhile, it can adapt to new situations and dynamically changing
environments by constantly learning from new data (Pan and Zhang 2022; Zhang et al. 2021). How­
ever, tunnel excavation process involves multifaceted geological conditions and engineering knowl­
edge that are difficult to directly obtain from data. Deep learning models rely on data for training,
and certain factors that significantly affect deformation may not be fully represented in the dataset.
Additionally, during tunnel excavation process, the effects of factors such as geological conditions,
excavation methods, and support parameters on the deformation of the excavation profile are com­
plex and intertwined. It is difficult to directly deduce the interactions and relationships between
these numerous factors from the data, resulting in challenges related to interpretability (Jong,
Ong, and Oh 2021; Li et al. 2024b; Zhu et al. 2024).
   In response to the above problems, this paper innovatively introduces the concept of knowledge and
proposes a data-knowledge hybrid driven intelligent prediction method of tunnel excavation contour
geometric deformation. Firstly, based on existing knowledge graph construction methods, combined
with the characteristics of tunnel excavation process data, a dynamic data-driven tunnel excavation
knowledge extraction method is proposed. Secondly, to clarify the numerous knowledge of tunnel exca­
vation process, a multi-process coupled tunnel excavation process organization framework is proposed,
forming the foundation for the tunnel excavation knowledge base construction. Finally, a method for
organizing deformation factors of tunnel excavation profiles is proposed to generate a deformation fea­
ture map. An integrated prediction model that combines asymmetric convolution and Transformer
modules is constructed to predict deformations, significantly improving prediction accuracy.

2. Methods
2.1. Framework
Figure 1 illustrates the overall framework of this paper, which comprises three main components: the
tunnel excavation data basis, the tunnel excavation knowledge basis, and the excavation profile defor­
mation prediction model. Firstly, the data basis consists of multi-source data, such as laser point
cloud, construction design parameters, construction measurement data. These data serve as the
input for tunnel identification and the prediction model. Secondly, the tunnel sections under exca­
vation are dynatableically identified based on the tunnel identification data, enabling the cyclic and
dynamic extraction of knowledge from the excavation scene. The tunnel excavation knowledge
base is constructed, and the knowledge is used as a guide to filter the prediction model input data.
Through the integration of data and knowledge, a hybrid-driven approach optimizes data acquisition
and updates the input dataset dynamically. Finally, Through the integration of data and knowledge, a
hybrid-driven approach optimizes data acquisition and updates the input dataset dynamically. a pre­
diction model combining asymmetric convolution and Transformer modules is established to calcu­
late the deformation prediction output. In summary, based on the method proposed in this paper, the
data-knowledge hybrid driven intelligent prediction of tunnel excavation profile deformation is rea­
lized, providing accurate data support for tunnel excavation control.


2.2 Dynamic data driven tunnel excavation knowledge extraction
Tunnel excavation is a continuous and repetitive process where different excavation sections
encounter varying construction environments. Segmenting and dynamically extracting knowledge
about tunnel excavation helps to fully consider the diverse impacts resulting from different exca­
vation conditions. This paper designs a dynamic data-driven knowledge extraction method. During
the forward excavation process, the dynamic storage and update of excavation knowledge is realized
in units of tunnel section.
    As shown in Figure 2, the process begins with the extraction of identification data from the tunnel
sections currently under excavation, such as section number, starting point, ending point, section
length. Based on this data, knowledge is extracted from each excavation scene and matched with
its corresponding scene. As the tunnel excavation progresses, the above process is applied to all
new section, enabling the dynamic data-driven knowledge extraction of tunnel excavation scene.

   Based on the above excavation process organization framework, a knowledge base is constructed

the structural basis for the knowledge base. Finally, with the above basic work, through the steps

2.4 Data-knowledge hybrid driven prediction of tunnel excavation profile deformation
2.4.1 Organization of deformation element
In predicting tunnel excavation profile deformation using deep learning, high-quality data is criti­
cal, as it directly affects the prediction’s accuracy and efficiency. Given the numerous factors that
influence tunnel profile deformation, it is essential to use effective methods to filter the input
data (Figure 5).
   Using the knowledge base constructed using the method described in Section 2.3, techniques
such as relevance analysis, centrality analysis, and influence propagation models are employed to
identify the most significant factors impacting deformation. This analysis filters out less influential
factors, simplifies input data, and optimizes the sample library for deformation prediction.
   In the subsequent deformation predictions, it is necessary to integrate multiple deformation
elements for feature extraction, including deformation values and related influencing factors. To
improve the efficiency of deformation feature extraction, this paper proposes a organization
method of tunnel excavation profile deformation factor. Firstly, the tunnel point cloud profile
lines are extracted in equal distances along the excavation direction. Each profile line is divided
into feature lines at equal angles. The angle of each point is extracted, and the point closest to
the feature line is the feature point. As shown in Figure 6.
   The tunnel surface space is mapped onto a two-dimensional plane, where each grid in the plane
grid corresponds to a feature point on the tunnel arc segment, creating a deformation feature map.
This realizes the mapping of the three-dimensional tunnel surface into the two-dimensional plane,
facilitating the extraction of deformation features using neural networks. Different deformation
elements are represented as separate channels in the feature map, with each grid storing the

2.4.2 Intelligent prediction of tunnel excavation profile geometric deformation
The deformation of tunnel excavation profile has both temporal and spatial characteristics during
tunnel excavation. Using a single model for prediction presents certain limitations. To address this,
an intelligent prediction model that can take into account both the temporal and spatial character­
istics of tunnel excavation profile deformation is proposed.




Figure 7. Schematic diagram of deformation element organization.
8        Y. HU ET AL.


    The deformation of tunnel excavation profile has feature correlations in the horizontal and ver­
tical directions. Horizontally, the deformation relationships between different feature points on the
same excavation profile. Vertically, the deformation relationship between feature points at similar
positions on successive profiles along the tunnel’s extension direction. Asymmetric convolution is
an efficient convolution operation. The use of asymmetric convolution kernels can extract the fea­
tures of the feature map in the horizontal and vertical directions respectively, enhancing the sensi­
tivity of the model to features in different directions (Ding et al. 2019). In this paper, asymmetric
convolution is cited, with convolution 1 × 3 convolution and 3 × 1 convolution decomposed from
3 × 3 convolution to extract the characteristic relationship of the horizontal and vertical defor­
mation of tunnel excavation respectively.
    The Transformer model employs a self-attention mechanism to analyse global temporal
relationships in sequence data, enabling efficient processing of complex spatial–temporal inter­
actions. Unlike the traditional RNN model, which rely on the output of the previous step, the Trans­
former model has higher parallel processing capabilities. For data such as tunnel profile
deformation prediction that contains complex spatiotemporal relationships, the Transformer
model is more efficient in sequence data processing (Sekhar and Dahiya 2023). Therefore, this
paper introduces the Transformer module to conduct an overall analysis of the tunnel excavation
profile deformation sequence data.
    The intelligent prediction model of tunnel excavation profile is illustrated in Figure 8. The input
consists of the deformation factor organization results described in Section 2.4.1. Firstly, asym­

3. Experiments and results
3.1 Case introduction
To verify the method proposed in this paper, a large-scale tunnel excavation project from an
ongoing high-speed railway construction site in the mountainous region of northwestern China
was selected for experimental analysis. The tunnel site area is a tectonic erosion low-medium moun­
tain and medium-mountain landform, with complex topography and landforms, steep terrain, deep
valleys. The surface vegetation is relatively well-developed, with generally thin soil cover, exposed
bedrock in some sections, and multiple areas of hazardous rock falls. The construction of this exper­
imental case area faces problems such as complex geological conditions, high risk level, strong
demand for technical measures, and great construction difficulty. The experimental case area is
shown in Figure 9.

4. Conclusions
The profile geometric deformation during tunnel excavation process involves numerous entities,
complex behaviours, and dynamic states. Traditional prediction methods of excavation profile
deformation often suffer from poor interpretability, limited comprehensiveness, and low accuracy.
By integrating the concept of digital twins with the tunnel excavation, the prediction accuracy of
tunnel profile deformations can be significantly enhanced. Therefore, this paper proposes a data-
knowledge hybrid driven intelligent prediction method of the tunnel excavation profiles geometric
deformation. A knowledge graph is introduced to enhance the interpretability of the various defor­
mation influencing factors during tunnel excavation process. Additionally, a novel method for con­
structing a knowledge base tailored to describe tunnel excavation profile deformations is designed
to better address the specific needs of the excavation process. Finally, by improving the existing
deep learning models and combining them with the data-knowledge hybrid driven method, the pre­
diction accuracy of tunnel profile geometric deformation is effectively increased. The main contri­
butions of this paper are specifically summarized as follows:
   Firstly, knowledge is introduced to describe the deformation-influencing factors of tunnel exca­
vation. Based on the tunnel section identification data, knowledge is extracted dynamically from
each tunnel section, enabling dynamic data-driven knowledge extraction of tunnel excavation scen­
arios. Secondly, a multi-process coupled tunnel excavation knowledge base construction method is
designed to describe the tunnel excavation process more completely and accurately. Additionally,
the data collection and acquisition of future tunnel sections can be guided by the above process,
achieving the integration effect of data and knowledge. Thirdly, the prediction model input is opti­
mized through a data-knowledge hybrid driven method. By combing asymmetric convolution and
Transformer models, an improved intelligent prediction model of tunnel excavation profiles geo­
metric deformation is constructed. According to the experimental results, the proposed method
shows improvements in MAE, MSE, and RMSE compared with the basic traditional methods
can reach 57.46%, 76.00%, and 53.12% respectively. Based on the above results, this paper presents
a method that can effectively improve the prediction accuracy of tunnel excavation profile geo­
metric deformation.
   The intelligent deformation prediction model proposed in this paper, based on convolutional
neural networks, is primarily used to extract tunnel excavation profile features. In future work,
this method can be extended to other similar application scenarios. For example, it could be adapted
for deformation scenarios of structures such as bridges and station buildings, the knowledge graph
construction and parameter settings can be adjusted according to the actual situation. Such exten­
sions would broaden the applicability of this method, making it highly valuable for deformation

monitoring across a wider range of fields. Additionally, parameters such as elastic modulus and
fracture development characteristics can be introduced. By employing rock mass experiments
and numerical simulation techniques, the dynamic variations of the physical and mechanical par­
ameters of surrounding rock under the influence of factors such as temperature, humidity, and in-
situ stress can be analysed. This enables the development of a more comprehensive deformation
prediction model.
